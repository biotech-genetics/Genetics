Abstract

In the present study the cytological and molecular effects of Glufosinate ammonium (GLA) on erythrocyte cells of Egyptian toads were studied. The Egyptian toads were treated with different concentration of GLA 3.7 mg/L, 12 mg/L and 15 mg/L for different exposure times (48 & 96 hours), Then micronuclear, nuclear abnormalities, and RAPD PCR profile were detected and scored for each treatment. The results revealed that GLA can increase the percentage of nuclear abnormalities as the time of treatment up to 96 h. Moreover, DNA samples treated with (GLA) showed polymorphic bands including loss of some of the bands which appear in the DNA samples of control toads. In conclusion amphibians have been considered as bio-indicators of aquatic and agricultural ecosystems. Also, several studies demonstrated that amphibians are sensitive organisms, suitable for detection of genotoxic agents. The current study revealed that Glufosinate ammonium is moderately toxic and provokes genomic damage in erythrocytes of Egyptian toads.
