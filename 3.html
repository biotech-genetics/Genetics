Abstract

This research aims to harness the diverse metabolic potential of different bacterial species to produce potent antimicrobial agents. Three bacterial species, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Pseudomonas fluorescens, and Lactococcus lactis, were selected as model organisms for their known antimicrobial potential. The study evaluates the antimicrobial activity of compounds produced by those bacteria, including Antibiotics (Niacin, Mupirocin) and Pyocyanin pigment. A significant antimicrobial activity of these compounds against a range of microbes tested. The antimicrobial activity confirmed these substances' potential to combat microbial infections effectively. Furthermore, this research contributes to expanding our understanding of bacterial diversity and its implications for antimicrobial production, with significant implications for the development of novel therapeutic strategies against microbial infections
