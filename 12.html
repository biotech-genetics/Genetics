Abstract

The purpose of this Study is to collect a number of fruit flies Drosophila sp. from the most exposed places to air pollution and undertake cytological analysis by examining the giant chromosome (the polytene) located in the salivary glands of the insect in the larval stage, comparing the chromosomes of the collected insects’ and the those of another population grown in pollution-free areas (the control sample). In order to observe any changes appearing in their offspring as a result of pollution, it could be morphological changes such as difference in wing shape, size, eye color or chromosomal changes like the presence of mutations such as additions, deletions, etc. Or the organism shows adaptation without any harm. A population of Drosophila is collected from one of the most areas exposed to air pollution in “Alexandria Governorate - Egypt. “Wadi El-Qamar”, in February 2024, finding two species of Drosophila sp.: Drosophila melanogaster, known to be settled in Alexandria, and another new species in the same area, Drosophila hydei. In order to examine the Polytene, through cytological techniques, the salivary glands of Drosophila hydei were isolated in the larval stage and a comparison between the collected sample and the control sample has been done looking for any changes or mutations appeared due to pollution. Following cytological examination, there was no difference between the chromosomes of both samples, referring to the absence of morphological or chromosomal changes in the offspring, indicating that the insect has shown a high adaptation to its surrounding environment.
