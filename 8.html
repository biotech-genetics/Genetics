Abstract

Onion (Allium cepa) is one of the most cultivated and widely grown vegetables in the world and it also has an important role in agriculture, nutrition and economy. Salinity is among the most devastating problems of agriculture and productivity of onion in many areas such as Egypt. Given that the maximum concentration of NaCl tolerated by the onion is 0.8 molar. The current study aimed to evaluate the effect of salinity on the growth and cytological aspects of onion. Different sodium chloride concentrations (0.05 g/mol, 0.1 g/mol and 0.6 g/mol) were used in the research. The findings revealed that as salt concentrations increased the percentage of abnormalities during cell division also rose. The highest percentage (10 %) of abnormality was recorded at concentration of 0.6 g/mol NaCl. However statistical analysis showed no significant difference in abnormalities among the salt concentrations tested. Various types of anomalies were identified, including sticky prophase, sticky anaphase, sticky a telophase, anaphase bridge, lagging chromosome, uncoiling DNA, C-metaphase, DNA fragments, adherent chromosome, sticky pro-metaphase, metaphase bridge and star metaphase. The results highlighted that salinity has adverse effects on the physiology of onions. Therefore, it is recommended to carry out more experiments to minimize the effect of salinity in order to maintain the productivity of onion in saline areas
