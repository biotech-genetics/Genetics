Abstract

Plastic waste has become a significant environmental issue due to its low degradation rate. Only a small percentage of plastic waste is currently being recycled, while the majority ends up in landfills and incineration. This has led to contamination of ecosystems on land and water. To address this problem, biodegradation of plastics using microorganisms and their enzymes has emerged as a sustainable alternative. This work aimed to test the ability of some bacterial strains, i.e. Pseudomonas sp. and Bacillus sp., to biodegrade plastic wastes. Collected plastic samples (yellow, black, and white plastic bags) were sterilized, cut into 3Ã—3 cm pieces, and weighed. Following that, three Winogradesky columns are created: one to test Pseudomonas aeruginosa, one to test Bacillus sp., and the third to test non-sterilized soil without the addition of any bacterial solution. Then they were gone for several days. Following these days, the plastic samples were weighed again to determine weight loss. Results showed that Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Bacillus sp., as well as non-sterilized soil, degrade plastic garbage with great efficiency. However, Pseudomonas aeruginosa degraded plastic higher than Bacillus sp. Also, non-sterilized soil has a similar impact as Bacillus sp.
